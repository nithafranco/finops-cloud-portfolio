# Inform — FinOps Concept 👀☁️

## Explaining Inform like you are 5 🧸

Imagine a house where everyone can order toys online.

One person buys blocks.
Another person buys crayons.
Someone else buys a puzzle.
And another person buys a huge teddy bear. Because apparently, we needed drama.

At the end of the month, one big bill arrives. 🧾

But there is a problem:

Nobody knows who bought what.

So everyone starts asking:

* Who bought the teddy bear?
* Why did the bill get bigger this month?
* Which toys belong to which person?
* Did we really need all of this?

That is where **Inform** comes in.

Inform is like putting a name label on each toy. 🏷️

Now we can see:

* These blocks belong to Ana
* These crayons belong to João
* This puzzle belongs to the Art team
* This very necessary teddy bear belongs to the Engineering team

Once everyone knows what belongs to them, they can understand their part of the bill.

They can also make better decisions next time.

Maybe they still need the teddy bear.
Maybe they bought too many crayons.
Maybe the puzzle was important.
Maybe something was bought by mistake.
Maybe someone just clicked too fast. It happens. Cloud is fun like that.

Inform does not tell people to stop using cloud.
It helps people understand what they are using, how much it costs and who owns it. ✨

---

## Now, the actual concept

In FinOps, **Inform** is the phase where we create visibility around cloud costs.

It helps teams understand:

* What they are spending
* Who owns each cost
* Where the cost is coming from
* What changed month over month
* How cloud usage impacts the bill

For me, Inform is basically about turning cloud billing data into something people can actually understand and use.

Because yes, technically the bill already has the information.
But does it make sense to normal humans? Not always. 🙂

---

## Why it matters

Before good cost allocation, it is hard to know **who is spending what**.

And without that, the conversation usually becomes:

> “Cloud is expensive.”

Very useful. Very deep. Very actionable. Obviously not.

Cloud costs can be spread across many accounts, projects, subscriptions, teams and services. Without visibility, Finance struggles to forecast, Engineering struggles to understand cost impact, and Leadership does not have a clear picture of what is happening.

Inform helps create that first layer of clarity in the middle of the cloud fog. 🌫️

It gives teams a way to see the impact of their decisions and start building accountability.

---

## Why I can talk about this

In a previous FinOps role, I worked with cost allocation across a multi-cloud environment, including AWS, Google Cloud, Azure and IBM Cloud.

The main goal was to map cloud resources to the correct owners and cost centres, including:

* AWS accounts
* Google Cloud projects
* Azure subscriptions

Every month, around the 5th business day, I reviewed newly created accounts, projects and subscriptions to make sure they had an owner and a cost centre.

When something was not clearly mapped, I investigated it by checking available information such as naming patterns, Slack channels, team names and, later, tagging information.

As the tagging process became more mature, tags and cost centre metadata became an important part of the investigation.

Basically: detective work, but instead of solving crimes, I was solving “who owns this cloud spend?” 🕵️‍♀️

---

## What this helped with

This process helped answer questions like:

* Who owns this cost?
* Which team or cost centre should receive this cloud spend?
* What changed compared to last month?
* Which account, project or subscription is driving the variation?
* Is this cost expected or should it be investigated?

Over time, this created more trust in FinOps reporting because the cost allocation process became a stronger base for analysis, forecasting and decision-making.

And trust matters a lot here.

If people do not trust the data, they will not trust the report.
If they do not trust the report, they will not act on it.
And then congratulations: we have a beautiful dashboard that nobody uses. 🫠

## Who used this information?

This information was useful for different teams:

* **FP&A / Finance:** used the data to support cloud cost provisions and month-end routines
* **Engineering:** used the data to understand allocated costs, investigate variations and think about optimisation opportunities
* **Leadership:** used the reporting to understand cloud spend by area and support decision-making
* **FinOps:** used the mapping as a foundation for visibility, governance and accountability

So yes, it started as “just mapping”.

But in practice, it became part of the foundation for better conversations between Finance, Engineering and Leadership.

---

## What I learned

Inform is not just reporting.

And it is definitely not just a spreadsheet sitting somewhere pretending to be governance.

It is the foundation that allows teams to trust the data, understand ownership and start having better conversations about cloud usage.

A good Inform process helps FinOps move from:

> “We have a cloud bill.”

to:

> “We understand who owns this cost, why it exists, what changed and what action may be needed.”

And that is where things start to get useful.

---

## Key takeaway ✨

Inform is the first step to make cloud costs visible, understandable and actionable for the right people.

Without visibility, teams cannot take ownership, because they may not even know they are spending.

With visibility, teams can start making better technical, financial and business decisions.

So, in short:

> No visibility, no ownership.
> No ownership, no optimisation.
> Just vibes and a very expensive cloud bill. ☁️💸
