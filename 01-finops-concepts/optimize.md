# Optimize — FinOps Concept 🛠️☁️

## Explaining Optimize like you are 7 🧸

In the **Inform** phase, we had a house where everyone was ordering toys online.

Thanks to Inform, we finally know who bought what.

Very mature. Very organised. Very Taurus energy. We love ownership.

But now we look at the toys and realise:

* Some toys are never used and are just living rent-free under the bed
* Some toys are way too big for what we need
* Some toys were bought twice because someone forgot to check the basket
* Some toys are still “on” for no reason, which is impressive because they are toys
* Some toys looked cheaper in the forecast, and then reality chose violence

That is where **Optimize** comes in.

Optimize is not about saying:

> “No more toys. Fun is cancelled.”

It is about asking:

> “Are we using the right things, in the right size, at the right time, for the right cost?”

Because sometimes the problem is not that we are using cloud.

The problem is that we are using cloud like someone left the lights on in every room, opened all the windows, ordered three pizzas, and then forgot they were not hungry.

Very cloud.
Very expensive.
Very preventable. 🫠

---

## Now, the actual concept

In FinOps, **Optimize** is the phase where teams identify and act on opportunities to reduce waste, improve efficiency and make better use of cloud resources.

It helps teams ask questions like:

* Are these resources still needed?
* Are they the right size?
* Are they being used efficiently?
* Can we reduce waste?
* Can we remove unused resources?
* Can we keep performance and reliability while spending better?

For me, Optimize is where visibility turns into action.

**Inform** says:

> “Here is what is happening.”

**Optimize** says:

> “Great. Now what are we doing about it?”

Because knowing the cloud bill is expensive is cute.

Doing something useful with that information is the plot.

---

## Why it matters

Cloud makes it very easy to create resources.

That is powerful.

That is also how companies end up paying for things nobody remembers creating.

A classic cloud love story.

Without optimisation, cloud environments can slowly collect waste over time, such as:

* Oversized resources
* Idle services
* Unattached storage
* Unused environments
* Poor commitment coverage
* Resources without clear ownership

Do not worry if some of these terms sound like mysterious cloud creatures.
We will meet them properly later. One monster at a time.

And the worst part?

Sometimes nobody notices because everything is technically “working”.

But “working” does not always mean “efficient”.

A room can have the lights on all day and still be technically working.

Your electricity bill, however, will have opinions.

Optimize helps teams reduce unnecessary cost while still protecting performance, reliability and business needs.

Because the goal is not to make cloud cheap at any cost.

The goal is to make cloud spend smarter.

## Why I can talk about this

In a previous FinOps role, I supported cloud cost optimisation across a multi-cloud environment, including AWS, Google Cloud, Azure and IBM Cloud.

My work involved helping technical teams understand optimisation opportunities, such as:

* Idle resources
* Resources that could potentially be stopped or removed
* Resources that needed an owner
* Opportunities that required technical validation
* Actions that needed approval from leaders or responsible teams

Basically, it was not just:

> “Here is a saving opportunity. Good luck.”

It was more like:

> “Here is the opportunity, here is the context, here is the possible impact, who owns it, who can validate it, and can we please not let this die in a spreadsheet?”

Because an optimisation opportunity that sits forever in a spreadsheet is not optimisation.

It is decoration.

A very expensive decoration.

---

## What this helped with

This process helped move optimisation from theory to action.

It helped answer questions like:

* What can be optimised?
* Who owns this resource?
* Is this resource still needed?
* Who needs to validate the change?
* Is there any risk in changing it?
* What is the expected impact?
* Was the action actually completed?

Over time, optimisation tracking helped connect cloud cost analysis with real actions from engineering and infrastructure teams.

It also helped create more accountability because savings were not just theoretical anymore.

They had owners, actions and follow-up.

A revolutionary concept: doing the thing after identifying the thing.

## What I learned

A good optimisation process needs:

* Visibility
* Ownership
* Technical validation
* Business context
* Prioritisation
* Follow-up
* Measurement of impact

The best optimisation work happens when teams do not feel attacked by the numbers.

Because nobody likes receiving a dashboard that basically says:

> “Hello, your service is expensive and suspicious.”

The goal is to create a conversation, not a crime scene.

---

## Key takeaway ✨

Optimize is where FinOps turns visibility into action.

It is about helping teams use cloud in a smarter, cleaner and more intentional way.

So, in short:

> No visibility, no ownership.
> No ownership, no optimisation.
> No optimisation, just expensive vibes and resources living their best life on your bill. ☁️💸
